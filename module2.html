Module 2: Prohibited Substances

What Are Prohibited Substances?

Prohibited substances are chemicals or drugs that athletes are not allowed to use in sports. These substances can enhance performance or cause harm, and they are banned to ensure fairness and safety in sports.

Categories of Prohibited Substances

  • Stimulants: Increase alertness and reduce fatigue. Examples: amphetamines, cocaine.
  • Anabolic Agents: Aid muscle growth and strength. Examples: anabolic steroids, hormones.
  • Diuretics: Increase urine production and can mask other banned substances. Example: furosemide.
  • Hormones: Enhance performance. Examples: growth hormones, erythropoietin (EPO).
  • Beta-2 Agonists: Treat asthma but can have performance-enhancing effects. Example: salbutamol.

Checking Prohibited Substances

Refer to the WADA Prohibited List to check substances that are banned. This list is updated annually.
